Baked Gao

This is my grandmother's original version of Chinese Nian Gao, a dessert traditionally served for Chinese New Years. While gao is usually steamed, this is her recipe for a baked version, and it also uses Hawaiian Ti leaves to line the pan and add flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 5 c. Mochiko rice flour
  • 2 c. water
  • 1 box brown sugar
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1/4 can sweet red beans (optional)
  • Sesame seeds, toasted
  • Ti leaves

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine Mochiko flour, water, brown sugar, coconut milk, and baking soda.
  2. Mix thoroughly with a wire whisk.
  3. Stir in red beans.
  4. Line an 8x12 inch pan with Ti leaves.
  5. Oil Ti leaves.
  6. Pour gao mixture into pan and sprinkle sesame seeds on top.
  7. Bake in a 350 degree F oven for 1 hour.
  8. Let cool, then slice into squares to serve.
